Part-Time Work & Side Hustles for College Students in Siliguri

  • siliguri
Post By Mehek Verma
Published May 15, 2025

Are you a student in Siliguri trying to balance classes, assignments, with the growing urge to earn your own money? You’re definitely not alone.

Whether it’s to pay for your expenses, build your skills, or simply become more independent, doing a part-time job or side hustle is one of the smartest things you can do while studying. And the good news? Siliguri is growing, and so are the opportunities — especially for students who are ready to learn and hustle.

“Opportunities don’t happen. You create them.” – Chris Grosser

In this blog, we’ll walk you through some of the best part-time jobs and freelance gigs you can start right here in Siliguri or North Bengal. Whether you’re into writing, marketing, teaching, or just exploring, we’ve got you covered.

Why Should Students Work Part-Time?

Working part-time is no more about earning pocket money alone, it helps you build real-world skills, gain independence, and discover what you actually enjoy doing.

Here’s what you gain:

  • Independence: No more waiting for allowances and help you build the feeling of independency within yourself.
  • Discover Your Career Path: Part-time jobs let you explore your passion early, which would be extremely beneficial for your career.
  • Real-World Learning: You’ll learn how the world operates and learn skills.
  • Skill Development: You will get the opportunity to build your talents in writing, design, communication.
  • Improved Resumes: Add actual work experience, that will increase your chance to get hired in future.
  • Develop Soft Skills: You’ll get to improve communication, time management, and professionalism.

Even working just a few hours a day can open doors — to income, skills, and long-term career growth.

Digital Marketing Intern

Every company now wants to be seen on Google, Facebook, and Instagram. That’s where digital marketing comes in. For example, a small bakery on Sevoke Road wants more customers, you, as a digital marketer, can help them post reels, run ads, and grow.

What You’ll Do:

  • Handle Instagram, Facebook and Google Business Profile
  • Shoot and edit reels
  • Schedule content using tools
  • Run and monitor ads on Google and Meta

Skills You Need:

  • Understanding social media
  • Using Canva and Meta & Google Ads Manager
  • Creativity and Content Writing

Where to Work:

  • Small businesses
  • Digital marketing agencies
  • Freelance on platforms like Internshala, Fiverr

How to Start:

  • Learn the basics from Youtube or take a short-term digital marketing course
  • Intern with a local brand and handle their social media
  • Offer to manage a friend’s or relative’s business page to gain practical experience

Graphic Design Intern

If you love experimenting with colors, making posters, or designing social media posts, graphic design is the best option for you and it can also help you to build a full time career as a graphic designer later on.

What You’ll Do:

  • Design logos, brochures, visiting cards
  • Make social media creatives

Skills You Need:

  • Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, Canva

Where to Work:

  • Local businesses
  • Event management companies
  • Share your work on Instagram or Behance to attract clients or internships.

How to Start:

  • Build a portfolio using free tools
  • Start designing for small brands
  • Pursue a short-term graphic design course

Videography & Photography

Siliguri has college events, pre-wedding shoots, and brand reels—all needing a good camera hand, so either you can take up small projects or assist professional videographers and photographers.

What You’ll Do:

  • Shoot photos/videos for brands, cafes, events
  • Edit videos and photos for reels

Skills You Need:

  • DSLR or smartphone camera
  • Tools like VN, InShot, Lightroom

Where to Work:

  • Wedding photographers
  • Restaurants and cafes
  • Influencers and creators

How to Start:

  • Learn the basics from Youtube or take a short-term video editing course
  • Offer services to friends or small cafes
  • Build a sample portfolio

Content Writer

If you enjoy writing blogs, captions, or stories, content writing is one of the easiest skills to monetize as brands are always in need of good content.

What You’ll Do:

  • Write blog posts, product descriptions, Instagram captions

Skills You Need:

  • Google Docs, Grammarly, Hemingway

Where to Work:

  • Digital marketing agencies
  • Local businesses

How to Start:

  • Start your own blog on Medium or WordPress
  • Use ChatGPT to help you write faster

Online or Offline Tutor

If you’re good at teaching or have strong knowledge in any subject, tutoring is a great side hustle. It is a flexible career, which will also help you to sharpen your own knowledge.

What You’ll Do:

  • Teach school students
  • Help with assignments and test preparation

Skills You Need:

  • Subject knowledge
  • Patience
  • Communication & explanation skills

Where to Work:

  • Create profile on UrbanPro, SuperProf
  • Ask around in your neighborhood

How to Start:

  • Start teaching one student
  • Charge hourly

Website Development Assistant

Most local businesses have websites or looking to have a website for their businesses or services, but they don’t know how to create and update them. That’s where you step in. As a student with basic website skills, you can assist them — and get paid for it.

What You’ll Do:

  • Upload blog posts or products
  • Create website from scratch
  • Change banners and images

Skills You Need:

  • Basic domain, hosting & WordPress knowledge

Where to Work:

  • Travel agencies
  • NGOs, news portals
  • Small businesses

How to Start:

  • Learn from YouTube tutorials or offline institutes like Braand School
  • Create a basic website on your own to showcase to clients

Event Management Staff

From college fests to weddings, event planners in Siliguri employ people for short period too, so you can apply for such positions.

What You’ll Do:

  • Help backstage, guest coordination, anchoring support

Skills You Need:

  • Management
  • Communication

Where to Work:

  • Event management companies in Siliguri

How to Start:

  • Network with event managers
  • Start on a project basis

Retail Assistant

Working in retail outlets is another good option as it can teach you valuable customer service skills and help you improve your communication.

What You’ll Do:

  • Handle counters, help with packaging

Skills You Need:

  • Basic customer communication

Where to Work:

  • Malls
  • Retail shops in the market

How to Start:

  • Apply to HR desk at stores
  • Look for positions on LinkedIn, Internshala etc.

Content Creation

If you are good in communication and love sharing ideas, experiences, or talents—whether it’s fashion, food, education, or travel, content creation can actually be a fun and rewarding side hustle.

What You’ll Do:

  • Make Instagram reels, YouTube vlogs, or write blogs based on your interest.
  • Collaborate with local brands or promote affiliate products.

Skills You Need:

  • Smartphone camera or basic filming setup
  • Basic editing skills using Canva, Filmora or CapCut
  • Creativity, storytelling

Where to Work:

  • Start your own Instagram/YouTube channel
  • Collaborate with cafes, local shops, or clothing stores in Siliguri

How to Start:

  • Choose a niche (fashion, food, travel, education, etc.)
  • Start posting 2-3 times a week
  • Join local creator communities

Where to Find These Jobs in Siliguri?

Looking for where to apply? Start here:

  • Instagram pages of local businesses
  • Facebook job groups (“Jobs in Siliguri”)
  • Referrals
  • Job apps like LinkedIn, Internshala, WorkIndia, Apna, Indeed

Final Checklist

Before jumping in, ask yourself a few questions to make sure the job is right for you:

  • Is this job safe and verified?
  • Are hours flexible for students?
  • Is there a chance to learn?
  • Are payments fair?
  • Does it match your career goals?

Always consult your mentor or parents before starting.

Siliguri is now a growing city full of talent and energy. Whether you want to become a freelancer, marketer, designer, or content writer, there is something for you.

But remember, every big journey starts with a small step. Choose a part-time role that excites you, learn from it, and build your career.
