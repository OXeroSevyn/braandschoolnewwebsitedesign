Digital Marketing Strategies for Offbeat Homestays in North Bengal

  • Blog
Post By braand
Published August 25, 2025

Running a homestay in North Bengal is not just about giving people a room to sleep. It is about opening your home, your kitchen, and even your heart to travelers who are looking for something different. More and more people today don’t want fancy hotels. They want a place that feels warm, local, and personal. That’s why the homestay business in North Bengal is growing so fast.

But here’s the real challenge. With so many homestays coming up, how do you make your place stand out? How do you reach the right guests without depending only on expensive booking websites that take away a big cut in commissions? The answer is simple: digital marketing. If you plan it right, digital marketing can bring you more bookings directly and help you build a brand of your own.

In this blog, some practical and simple digital marketing strategies have been shared for homestays in North Bengal. These are not just random tips but proven methods that can help you attract more guests and grow your business in 2025 and beyond.

Why Homestays in North Bengal Are Growing?

Tourism in North Bengal is not new. Darjeeling, Kalimpong, Dooars – all these places have been attracting tourists for decades. But what’s happening in the last few years is something different. People no longer want only hotels, they want to experience how locals live, eat home-cooked local food, and get tips for offbeat places that are not in guidebooks.

  • According to the Ministry of Tourism, domestic travel in India grew by almost 60% in the last five years.
  • A big part of this growth came from homestays and vacation rentals.
  • A survey by Booking.com found that 73% of Indian travelers in 2024 preferred stays that connect them with the local community.
  • Between 2022 and 2024, India saw an 85% growth in homestays compared to only 25% growth in hotels.

North Bengal is a clear leader in this. Tourism minister Indranil Sen even shared that nearly 65% of West Bengal’s registered homestays are in North Bengal alone. Darjeeling has 248 registered homestays, Kalimpong has over 1000, and the numbers are still rising. Every year, about 6 lakh Indian tourists and 30,000 foreign nationals visit Darjeeling district itself.

And the money side? The Indian homestay market was valued at USD 540 million in 2024 and is expected to grow at a 11% CAGR till 2029. By 2025, the size may touch USD 600 million. Clearly, this is the best time to run a homestay in North Bengal.

Why Digital Marketing Matters for Homestays

Now here’s the problem. Most homestay owners depend heavily on OTAs (online travel agencies) like Booking.com, Airbnb, or MakeMyTrip. While these platforms bring visibility, they also take away 15–20% of your booking amount as commission. That’s a big cut.

Digital marketing gives you the power to reach travelers directly. With the right strategy, you can:

  • Get bookings without paying high commissions.
  • Build your own brand, not just be another listing.
  • Stay connected with past guests so they return or recommend you.

Let’s now go step by step into the most effective digital marketing strategies for homestays in North Bengal.

1. Website Optimization 

Think of your website as your digital homestay. If it looks messy or loads slowly, guests will leave without booking.

  • User-friendly design: Keep the website clean, simple, with easy navigation. Guests should find your rooms, prices, and contact details easily and quickly.
  • SEO for local search: Use keywords like “homestays in Darjeeling”, “Kalimpong Homestay”, or “offbeat homestays North Bengal”. This will help your site show up when people search on Google.
  • Mobile-friendly: Most tourists use phones to check and book stays. So make sure that your website is responsive and works smoothly on mobile.
  • Fast loading: A slow website kills interest. Use compressed images and a good hosting service.

2. Content Marketing 

People love stories. Don’t just sell rooms; sell experiences.

  • Blogging: Write simple blogs about local attractions – like “5 Best Village Walks in Kalimpong” or “Hidden Tea Gardens in Darjeeling”. This helps your website rank on Google and shows travelers you know the place well.
  • Storytelling: Share small stories of your guests, your family recipes, or your homestay’s history.
  • Local flavor: Create content around festivals, culture, and food of North Bengal. Tourists love this.

3. Social Media Marketing 

Most travelers today check Instagram before they check hotel websites. Social media is where you can shine.

  • Instagram & Facebook: Firstly,optimize your social media accounts properly, then post content regularly. Share real photos and reels of your homestay, the mountains, local food, cultural experiences and customer testimonials. And also engage with your followers regularly,reply to comments, answer queries fast, and use Instagram Stories to connect.
  • Influencer Marketing: Another important way is to partner with travel vloggers and influencers. Invite them to stay at your homestay in exchange for honest reviews, reels, and posts. When they share their experience, it reaches thousands of potential guests who already love travel content. This kind of authentic promotion feels natural, not forced, and can bring you direct bookings.

4. Online Reviews

A traveler will always check reviews before booking and it is basically your digital reputation.

  • Ask for reviews: Politely request guests to leave a Google review before they leave as it will enhance your credibility.
  • Reply quickly: Thank people for good reviews, and handle negative ones politely.
  • Show reviews: Add top reviews on your website and social media.

5. Paid Advertising 

If you want to increase visibility, google and meta ads can help.

  • Google Ads: Invest in google ads targeting keywords like “homestays in North Bengal” or “Darjeeling Homestays”etc. that your prospective customers might search for.
  • Facebook & Instagram Ads: Run campaigns with beautiful visuals. Highlight special offers like “Winter Discount” or “Tea Garden Stay” and any other unique features that you would like to offer, so that you differentiate from your competitors.

6. Email Marketing 

Not everyone books on the first visit. Some people explore, leave, and come back later. Email helps you stay in their memory.

  • Build a list: Offer small travel guides like “Hidden Gems of Kalimpong” in exchange for their email.
  • Newsletters: Send simple updates once a month like local festivals, offers, or guest stories, that will keep your homestay in their memory.
  • Exclusive offers: Give discounts for repeat guests or early bookings through email.

7. Digital Collaborations With Local DMCs & Travel Agencies

Many travel agencies and DMCs in Siliguri & North Bengal have strong digital presence on Instagram pages, YouTube channels, WhatsApp groups, blogs, and tour packages on their websites. Collaborating with them can increase your visibility instantly.

Partner with them for digital promotions, package listing, reels, and cross-posts. When they include your homestay in their itineraries or feature you on their social media, you get direct reach to travelers actively planning a trip and you will also get to make long-term partnerships.

As the number of homestays are growing gradually in North Bengal, to survive in this growing market, you need to start digital marketing. Start small like maybe a simple website and an Instagram page, then slowly add blogs, start ads, and try influencer marketing. Within months, you’ll see more direct bookings and more recognition for your brand.

Be consistent and in 2025, when more travelers are looking for authentic stays, your homestay in North Bengal can be their first choice.
